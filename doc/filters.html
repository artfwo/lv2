Frequency response of a brick-wall filter

[Widipedia:] The frequency response \(H\) of a "brick-wall filter" is the rectangular function: \begin{equation} H(f)=rect\left(\frac{f}{2B}\right), \end{equation} where \(f\) denotes frequency, \(B\) is the cutoff frequency ("bandwidth") and the rectangular function \(rect\) is defined as \begin{equation} rect(x)=\left\{\begin{aligned} 1&\quad\text{if}\quad|x|\lt \frac{1}{2}\\ \frac{1}{2}&\quad\text{if}\quad|x|= \frac{1}{2}\\ 0&\quad\text{if}\quad|x|\gt \frac{1}{2}.\\ \end{aligned} \right. ï¿¼ \end{equation}

Impulse response of a brick-wall filter

[Wikipedia:] The Fourier transform of the \(rect\) function is \begin{equation} \mathcal{F}(rect(f))=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}rect(f)\,e^{-i2\pi ft}\,df=sinc(t), \end{equation} where \(sinc\) is the normalized sinc function defined as \begin{equation} sinc(x)=\frac{sin(\pi x)}{\pi x}. \end{equation}

Lemma. Let \(\mathcal{F}(g(f))=h(t)\) and \(\tilde{g}(f)\equiv g(cf)\). Then \(\mathcal{F}(\tilde{g}(f))=\frac{1}{c}h\left(\frac{t}{c}\right)\).

Argument: \begin{equation} \mathcal{F}(\tilde{g}(f))=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}g(cf)\,e^{-i2\pi ft}\,df= \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}g(cf)\,e^{-i2\pi fc\frac{t}{c}}\frac{1}{c}\,d(cf)=\frac{1}{c}h\left(\frac{t}{c}\right) \end{equation}

Applying the lemma to the frequency response of the brick-wall filter yields the impulse response \(h(t)\) of the brick-wall filter [c.f. Wikipedia] \begin{equation} h(t)\equiv\mathcal{F}(H(f))=2B\frac{\sin(2B\pi t)}{2B\pi t}=2B\,sinc(2B t) \end{equation}
